## An AI sidebar that actually respects you

Inquiso is an open-source, cross-browser extension (Chrome, Firefox, and Chromium-based
browsers) that reads the page you're on — or a whole tab group, or every tab in a window — and
lets you ask, summarize, assess, and act, without ever leaving the page.

<CardGrid stagger>
  <Card title="Ask" icon="comment">
    “What does this page say about refunds?” Get grounded answers with the page as context —
    no copy-pasting into a separate tab.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Summarize" icon="document">
    “Give me the 5 key points.” Condense long articles, docs, and threads across a single page
    or an entire window of tabs.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Assess" icon="approve-check">
    “Is this source trustworthy? What's the bias?” Weigh credibility before you trust what
    you're reading.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Act" icon="rocket">
    “Fill this form” or “find the cheapest option and add it to cart.” Watch the agent work —
    sensitive actions always require your confirmation.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

## Built on principles, not lock-in

<CardGrid>
  <Card title="Bring your own model" icon="setting">
    Run free and fully local with Chrome Built-in AI (Gemini Nano), or bring your own key for
    OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, or Qwen. You stay in control of cost and data.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Transparent agent" icon="seti:plan">
    Every reasoning step and every action is shown to you in real time. Nothing sensitive or
    irreversible happens without an explicit confirmation — enforced in the background, not by
    the model.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Local-first & serverless" icon="laptop">
    There is no Inquiso backend, no telemetry, and no sync. Your data goes only to the AI
    provider you choose — nowhere else.
  </Card>
  <Card title="MemoryAgent" icon="star">
    Inquiso remembers *how* tasks were completed — typed, scoped, inspectable memory and
    learned browser skills that live entirely on your machine, and can never authorize an
    action on their own.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

## Private and secure by design

Manifest V3, a locked-down CSP, least-privilege permissions, and encrypted key storage.
Page content is treated as untrusted data, never as instructions — with first-class
prompt-injection defenses (0% injection success in the adversarial memory suite).
